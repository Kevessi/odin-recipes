Carbonara Recipe

Description

Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish that brings together simple yet luxurious ingredients like eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. Whether born from the ingenuity of World War II soldiers or the traditional recipes of Roman shepherds, this dish is a culinary icon that celebrates the magic of a few quality ingredients. No cream, onions, or garlicâ€”just straightforward, hearty Italian cooking.

Ready to learn how to make this delicious recipe quickly and easily? Let's get cooking!

Ingredients List

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente.
  3. In a separate pan, cook the diced pancetta over medium heat until crispy.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and a generous pinch of black pepper.
  5. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  6. Add the hot pasta to the pancetta pan and mix well.
  7. Remove the pan from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture.
  8. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach the desired sauce consistency.
  9. Season with a pinch of salt and more black pepper to taste.
  10. Serve immediately with additional Pecorino Romano cheese on top.
